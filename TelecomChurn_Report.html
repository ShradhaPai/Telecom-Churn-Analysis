01 — Objective

What We Set Out to Answer

Customer churn is the silent revenue drain in the telecom industry. Every churned customer represents not just lost recurring revenue, but the cost of failed acquisition — and the far higher cost of winning them back.

This analysis cuts through 7,043 subscriber records to find the patterns that separate customers who stay from those who leave — and translate those patterns into actionable retention levers.

Business Context: Acquiring a new customer costs 5–7× more than retaining an existing one. Even a 5% improvement in retention can increase profitability by 25–95% (Harvard Business Review).

Research Questions

→ Which contract types are most strongly linked to churn?
→ Does payment method signal churn likelihood?
→ Do value-added services (security, backup, support) reduce churn?
→ How does customer tenure affect churn probability?
→ Which customer demographic segments are most at risk?

03 — Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

📋
Contract Type is the Strongest Churn Signal
Month-to-month subscribers churn at dramatically higher rates than annual or two-year contract holders. The pattern is stark and consistent — as contract commitment increases, churn falls sharply. Long-term contracts create switching friction and reflect genuine customer satisfaction.
HIGH IMPACT
💳
Electronic Check = Highest Churn Risk
Customers paying via electronic check show the highest churn rate of all payment methods. Those enrolled in automatic payment methods — bank transfer auto-pay and credit card auto-pay — are significantly more loyal. Auto-pay is a proxy for deeper engagement and reduces billing friction.
HIGH IMPACT
🌐
Fiber Optic Users Churn More Than DSL
Counterintuitively, subscribers on the premium fiber optic internet plan churn at higher rates than DSL customers. This likely signals unmet expectations around price-to-performance — customers paying premium prices expect premium quality and service reliability.
MEDIUM IMPACT
🛡️
Value-Added Services Are Retention Anchors
Across four separate services — tech support, online security, online backup, and device protection — the pattern is identical: customers without these add-ons churn at roughly double the rate of those who have them. Each additional service bundled acts as a retention layer.
HIGH IMPACT
⏱️
Month One is the Danger Zone
Churn is highest in the first month of subscription and declines steeply with tenure. Long-term customers (2+ years) almost never leave. The early customer experience is the single most critical period for retention investment — what happens in the first 90 days determines the lifetime of the relationship.
HIGH IMPACT
👨‍👩‍👧
Family Structure Anchors Customers
Customers with dependents are significantly less likely to churn. Those without partners also show elevated churn risk. Family plans create multi-user dependency that makes switching costly and disruptive, acting as a natural retention mechanism independent of pricing.
BEHAVIORAL INSIGHT

05 — Recommendations

What the Business Should Do

01
Launch a First-90-Day Retention Program
Month-one churn is the highest in the entire customer lifecycle. Dedicated onboarding, proactive check-ins, and early value demonstration in the first 90 days will have the highest ROI of any retention investment. Identify customers approaching month 3 without engaging additional services as immediate intervention targets.
Critical
02
Incentivize Auto-Pay Enrollment at Signup
Electronic check customers are the highest churn cohort. Offering a small monthly discount (even ₹50–100) for auto-pay enrollment would reduce churn risk while also reducing billing operations cost. Frame it as a benefit, not a nudge.
Critical
03
Bundle Value-Added Services into Onboarding Packages
Tech support, online security, online backup, and device protection each independently halve churn rates. Rather than upselling them individually post-signup, include them in a 3-month free trial bundle at onboarding. Customers who use these services are far less likely to leave.
Critical
04
Audit Fiber Optic Pricing and Service Quality
Fiber optic subscribers — who pay the most — churn at the highest rates. This paradox signals a price-to-value mismatch. Conduct targeted surveys among fiber customers and compare complaint rates, downtime incidents, and speed consistency against DSL benchmarks before the next price revision cycle.
Medium
05
Design Family and Multi-User Plans to Capture Single Subscribers
Customers with dependents are significantly more loyal. Single customers without partners represent the highest flight risk. Offering shareable plan options or referral benefits that bring their social network onto the platform can convert single-user relationships into stickier multi-user ones.
Medium
