Telecom Customer Churn — Data Visualizations
Shradha R Pai  ·  IBM Telco Dataset  ·  7,043 records  ·  Python (Pandas, Seaborn)  ·  Jupyter Notebook
01 — Contract & Payment Factors
Churn by contract type
Month-to-month: high churn. Two year: very low.
Retained Churned
Key finding: Month-to-month subscribers churn at ~42% vs only 3% for two-year contracts.
Churn by payment method
Electronic check: highest churn. Auto-pay: lowest.
Retained Churned
Key finding: Electronic check users churn at ~45% vs ~15% for auto-pay methods.
02 — Internet Service
Churn by internet service type
Fiber optic: highest churn. No internet: lowest.
Retained Churned
Key finding: Despite being the premium offering, fiber optic subscribers churn at ~42% — more than twice the DSL rate (~19%). Suggests unmet price-to-value expectations among high-paying customers.
03 — Customer Demographics
Churn by partner status
No partner: higher churn.
Retained Churned
Key finding: Single customers (no partner) churn at ~33% vs ~20% for those with partners.
Churn by dependents
No dependents: high churn. With dependents: very low.
Retained Churned
Key finding: Customers with dependents churn at only ~12% vs ~31% for those without — family plans are natural retention anchors.

05 — Tenure Effect
Churn rate vs customer tenure (months)
Churn peaks in month 1 and steadily declines to near-zero at 60+ months.
Key finding: Churn is highest in the first month and declines sharply with tenure. Customers who survive 24+ months almost never leave. This makes the first 90 days the highest-priority window for retention investment.
